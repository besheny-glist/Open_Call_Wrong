OPEN-CALL
Memory Pavilion @ The Wrong Biennale
Deadline: 29 July 2025

Who We Are Looking For
We are looking for 8 visual artists whose work explores memory, grief, and loss – whether deeply personal or culturally collective.
We welcome submissions that reflect on:
– individual or collective memory
– family archives, loss, and identity
– rituals of mourning and remembrance
– the emotional and physical spaces of absence

You can work with photography, AI-generated imagery, collage, video, sound, or interactive forms, 3D art

About the Pavilion
Memory Pavilion is a curated digital pavilion for The Wrong Biennale 2025–2026.
Artists' works will be reimagined in an immersive virtual environment, where visitors will navigate dreamlike rooms filled with abstract photographs, AI-generated visuals, and interactive 3D elements derived from memory.

We will be integrating:
– AI image transformation (diffusion models trained on memory-related datasets)
– image-to-3D sculptural objects
– generative environments that evolve through interaction and movement

The exhibition launches online in November 2025 and will run through March 2026.

How to Apply
Please send the following to memorypavilion@gmail.com
by 29 July 2025:
– Project description (max. 300 words)
– Artist bio or statement (max. 200 words)
– 3–6 images or video links (can be sketches, WIP, or final work)
– Optional: a few lines about your relationship to memory or loss

Technical requirements: Final chosen images should be Hi-res

There is no application fee. Selected artists will be featured in the pavilion, credited as collaborators, and supported in adapting their work to the digital format.

Questions?
